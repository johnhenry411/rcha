    About us

    RCHA was established to address Healthy Ageing challenges that older persons aged 60+ face

    Together with others in the population ageing space, we promote healthy ageing. Our multi-prong approaches serve to empower individuals and communities; strengthen systems and institutions for Healthy Ageing work; increase access of older persons to healthy ageing services; and generate evidence. We empower older persons to lead active, fulfilling lives while ensuring their rights, safety, security and social connectedness. We are committed to building communities and environments that recognize and respect the rights, needs, and contributions of older persons.

    Our Vision

    A world where every older person experiences and enjoys healthy ageing.

    Our mission

    To promote Healthy Ageing by empowering individuals and communities, strengthening systems and generating evidence.

    Facts and Figures

    The Human Population is Ageing.
    By 2030, 1 in 6 people in the world will be aged 60 years or over. At this time the share of the population aged 60+ will increase from 1 billion in 2020 to 1.4 billion. By 2050, the world’s population of people aged 60+ will double (2.1 billion). Kenya’s population of older persons aged 60+ is projected to reach 9.4 million in 2050, up from 2.7 million in 2019.
    October 15, 2020
    Increasing cases of Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs) and Premature Death.
    60% of older persons have at least one NCD, and 20% have three or more; without early care, these conditions cause severe complications and costly disability. Globally, NCDs account for 71% of deaths, and in Kenya, over 50% of hospital admissions and 55% of premature deaths, underscoring the need for targeted interventions.
    October 15, 2020
    Access to Healthy Ageing services is poor

    Health systems are disease oriented and often lack the resources and infrastructure needed to address the complex health needs of the older persons leaving them underserved Many African countries have not prioritized healthy ageing in their health and development agendas. Health systems often lack the resources and infrastructure needed to address the complex health needs of the older persons.

    October 15, 2020
    Abuse of elder persons.
    The abuse of older people, also known as Elder Abuse, is a single or repeated act, or lack of appropriate action, occurring within any relationship where there is an expectation of trust, which causes harm or distress to an older person. This type of violence constitutes a violation of human rights and includes physical, sexual, psychological and emotional abuse; financial and material abuse; abandonment; neglect; and serious loss of dignity and respect.

    Around 1 in 6 people 60 years and older experienced some form of abuse in community settings during the past year.
    Rates of abuse of older people are high in institutions such as nursing homes and long-term care facilities, with 2 in 3 staff reporting that they have committed abuse in the past year.
    Rates of abuse of older people have increased during the COVID-19 pandemic.
    Abuse of older people can lead to serious physical injuries and long-term psychological consequences.
    Abuse of older people is predicted to increase as many countries are experiencing rapidly ageing populations
    October 15, 2020

    Our work

    At RCHA our work is hinged on four complementary program pillars:

    Service Delivery
    Actions and activities that directly or indirectly increase access to healthy ageing services. We focus on facilitating Integrated Care for Older Persons (ICOPE), Universal Health Coverage (UHC), Long-Term Care (LTC) and Community Based Approaches (CBA)
    Empowerment
    Information, education, and opportunities to enhance the capacities of individuals and communities for Healthy Ageing. We sensitize communities on healthy ageing, support organization development, and train communities on advocacy and policy influencing.
    Systems Strengthening
    Under this pillar RCHA provides thought leadership, technical resources and support, advocacy, and training to improve institutional capacities for healthy ageing
    Evidence
    We undertake monitoring, Evaluation, Accountability, Research, and Learning (MEARL) activities to generate knowledge and evidence to inform healthy ageing policy and programming.
